The **Clinical History** (Ficha Clínica) provides a unified interface for documenting both hereditary-familial history and pathological history in a single comprehensive form. This collapsible section-based design allows efficient data entry while maintaining organization.

## Overview

The Clinical History form is divided into two main sections:

1. **Heredo-familial history** (Antecedentes Heredo-Familiares)
2. **Pathological history** (Antecedentes Patológicos)

Each section can be expanded or collapsed independently, allowing you to focus on specific areas during data entry.

<Note>
A patient must be selected before you can access and edit the Clinical History form.
</Note>

## Section 1: Heredo-familial history

This section documents the health status and conditions of the patient's family members to identify hereditary risk factors.

### Family members tracked

The form includes predefined rows for:
- **Madre** (Mother)
- **Abuela Materna** (Maternal Grandmother)
- **Abuelo Materno** (Maternal Grandfather)
- **Padre** (Father)
- **Abuela Paterna** (Paternal Grandmother)
- **Abuelo Paterno** (Paternal Grandfather)
- **Hermanos** (Siblings)

<Info>
You can add additional relatives beyond the standard family tree using the "Mostrar Más Parientes" option.
</Info>

### Family member fields

For each family member, document:

| Field | Description | Options |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Estado Vital** | Current vital status | Vivo/Viva, Fallecido/Fallecida |
| **Edad** | Current age or age at death | Numeric value |
| **Patologías** | Diagnosed conditions | Multi-select from comprehensive list |
| **Observaciones** | Additional notes | Free text |

### Adding extra relatives

<Steps>
  <Step title="Show additional relatives section">
    Click the **"Mostrar Más Parientes"** button below the main family table.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add a relative">
    Click **"Agregar Otro Pariente"** to create a new row.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter relationship">
    Specify the relationship (e.g., "Tío", "Prima", "Sobrino").
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete the information">
    Fill in vital status, age, pathologies, and observations as needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Section 2: Pathological history

This section documents the patient's own medical history, including chronic diseases, surgeries, hospitalizations, and more.

### Chronic diseases (Enfermedades Crónicas)

Select from common chronic conditions:
- Diabetes Tipo 1, Diabetes Tipo 2
- Hipertensión Arterial
- Asma, EPOC
- Epilepsia
- Cardiopatías, Artritis Reumatoide
- Osteoartritis, Osteoporosis
- Hipotiroidismo, Hipertiroidismo
- Enfermedad Renal Crónica
- Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C
- VIH/SIDA

A free-text field is available for "Otras enfermedades crónicas" not in the predefined list.

### Previous surgeries (Cirugías Previas)

For each surgery, record:
- **Type**: Name of the surgical procedure (e.g., "Apendicectomía", "Cesárea")
- **Date**: When the surgery was performed
- **Notes/Complications**: Any relevant details or post-operative complications

<Info>
Click the **"Agregar Cirugía"** button to add multiple surgical history entries.
</Info>

### Hospitalizations (Hospitalizaciones)

Document past hospital admissions:
- **Reason**: Why the patient was hospitalized
- **Date**: Date of admission
- **Duration**: Length of hospital stay
- **Notes**: Additional relevant information

Use the **"Agregar Hospitalización"** button to add multiple hospitalization records.

### Additional fields

| Field | Purpose |
|-------|----------|
| **Allergies** | Allergies to medications, materials (latex), anesthetics |
| **Transfusions** | Blood transfusion history and reactions |
| **Immunization status** | Complete, Incomplete, Unknown, N/A |
| **Immunization details** | Specific vaccines received and dates |
| **Current medications** | List of medications with dosages |
| **Toxic habits** | Smoking, alcohol, drugs |
| **General notes** | Any additional relevant information |

## Visual organization

The form uses color-coding for family lineages:
- **Pink background** (#ffe6e6): Maternal line (Madre, Abuela Materna, Abuelo Materno)
- **Blue background** (#e6f3ff): Paternal line (Padre, Abuela Paterna, Abuelo Paterno)
- **Orange background** (#fff3e6): Siblings (Hermanos)

This visual distinction helps quickly identify family lineage when reviewing data.

## Saving the clinical history

Click the **"Guardar Antecedentes"** button at the bottom of each section to save the entered information.

<Warning>
Make sure to save each section separately. Changes in the Heredo-familial section require clicking its save button, and the Pathological section has its own save button.
</Warning>

## Best practices

### Collecting family history
- Interview the patient about known family medical conditions
- Focus on first-degree relatives (parents, siblings) first
- Document age at diagnosis for hereditary conditions
- Note any patterns of disease across generations

### Recording pathological history
- Be thorough but concise
- Use standard medical terminology
- Include dates whenever possible
- Update regularly as new information becomes available
